PhD Student in Computer and Information Science

University of Pennsylvania · Advised by M. Ani Hsieh

My research interests lie at the intersection of dynamical systems, robotics, and machine learning. I work on understanding and predicting complex nonlinear dynamics, with applications ranging from bifurcation analysis using transformers to gait optimization in quadruped robots.

I am broadly interested in developing data-driven methods that bridge the gap between theoretical dynamical systems analysis and real-world robotic applications.

Research

⚙

Dynamical Systems

Using transformer architectures to reliably infer bifurcation diagram structure from as few as 30 noisy trajectories, enabling prediction of critical transitions in nonlinear systems without traditional continuation methods.

🤖

Robotics

Optimizing quadruped locomotion using central pattern generators to achieve smooth gait transitions with minimized joint torque. Optimal gaits conform to biological observations of feline species, applied to planetary rover designs.

👁

Computer Vision

Exposing AP's blindness to duplicate predictions in instance segmentation and proposing Semantic Sorting + NMS, achieving a 2x F1 improvement, up to 33x reduction in duplicate confusion, and 6x faster inference than Mask NMS (CVPR 2023).
