PhD Student in Computer and Information Science

University of Pennsylvania · Advised by M. Ani Hsieh

I am a PhD student at the University of Pennsylvania studying computer and information science. My research interests lie at the intersection of dynamical systems, robotics, and machine learning. I work on understanding and predicting complex nonlinear dynamics, with applications ranging from bifurcation analysis using transformers to gait optimization in quadruped robots.

Previously, I completed my B.A.Sc. in Engineering Science at the University of Toronto, where my thesis focused on minimum torque gait transitions in quadruped rovers using central pattern generators.

Research

⚙

Dynamical Systems

Understanding and predicting bifurcations and nonlinear dynamics using data-driven approaches, including transformer architectures for inferring bifurcation diagrams.

🤖

Robotics

Gait optimization and locomotion control for quadruped robots, including work on central pattern generators for smooth gait transitions with minimal torque.

👁

Computer Vision

Developing better evaluation metrics for instance segmentation and exploring vision-based perception systems for real-world applications.
